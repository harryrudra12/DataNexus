# DataNexus — LinkedIn Profile Optimization Checklist

**Why this matters:** Every prospect you DM or email will check your LinkedIn within 30 seconds. If your profile says "Software Engineer at TCS, 2018-2024" they will not respond. If it tells the DataNexus story clearly, they will. This is a 90-minute investment that doubles your reply rate.

Do this **before** you send the first outreach message.

---

## ✓ The 7 things to fix today

### 1. Headline (the most important field)

❌ Don't write: `Software Engineer | Data Engineer | Hadoop | Spark`

✅ Write: `Building DataNexus — open-source data platform for India's DPDP Act 2023 | Hyderabad | Founder`

**Why:** A prospect reading "Software Engineer" assumes you are job-hunting. A prospect reading "Building DataNexus" assumes you are someone they should know. The headline sets expectations for everything else.

### 2. Profile photo

- Clean, professional headshot
- Eyes visible, slight smile
- Plain background (not a busy room or wedding crop)
- Not the LinkedIn default silhouette
- Not a wedding photo cropped to your face

If you don't have one: any modern phone in good light against a plain wall takes a fine photo. Cost: ₹0.

### 3. Cover banner

Use a custom banner that says something. Don't leave it as the default LinkedIn blue.

Free options:
- Canva (free tier): "DataNexus banner LinkedIn template"
- Or simple text on a navy background: **"Building India's open-source data platform · datanexus.io"**

### 4. About section (300 words max)

The opening 2 sentences must hook the reader. Then 3 paragraphs:

```
[Sentence 1: One-line problem statement]
India's DPDP Act 2023 forces every Indian company to prove citizen-data
compliance to regulators. AWS, GCP, and Azure structurally cannot.

[Sentence 2: One-line solution statement]
DataNexus is the open-source data platform that solves this — with
Hyperledger blockchain proof every regulator can verify in 60 seconds.

[Paragraph 1: Why you]
I founded DataNexus in [year] after [N] years of building data
infrastructure at [previous companies]. I saw firsthand how Indian
enterprises struggle to balance cloud convenience with data sovereignty.
DataNexus is the answer I wish I had at [previous role].

[Paragraph 2: What you do today]
We are currently in customer discovery — talking to CTOs and CDOs at
Indian banks, hospitals, and government departments to understand
their real DPDP and data engineering pain. If you are dealing with
DPDP audit pressure, I would love 15 minutes of your time.

[Paragraph 3: What's next]
Open-source code at [github URL when you have it]. Pilot program
running through 2025. Currently raising a seed round.

Reach me: [email] · [phone] · datanexus.io
```

Add a current role:

- **Description:**

```
DataNexus is open-source data infrastructure for India.

Currently in customer discovery. If you lead data engineering or compliance

datanexus.io · [email]
```

### 6. Featured section — pin one post

Pin the founding-story post you'll publish in Action 9. Don't pin generic articles. Pin YOUR thinking.

### 7. Activity — start posting once a week

Topics that resonate with Indian enterprise CTOs:

- DPDP Act technical breakdown ("What DPDP-001 actually means for your data architecture")
- Cost comparisons ("How we got 67% lower cloud costs with open-source")
- Indian language NLP examples ("Telugu queries in 200ms — here's how")
- Honest founder updates ("3 things I got wrong this week")

**One post per week. Every week. For 6 months.** That builds the credibility that makes outreach replies happen.

## ✓ Before sending your first DM, verify:

- [ ] Headline says "Building DataNexus" or equivalent founder framing
- [ ] Profile photo is clean and professional
- [ ] About section opens with the problem, not your background
- [ ] Current Experience shows DataNexus as your role today
- [ ] Featured section has at least one DataNexus post or article pinned
- [ ] Contact info is set: email, datanexus.io URL
- [ ] You have at least 100 connections (add CAs, college alumni, Indian tech founders)
- [ ] You have posted at least one DataNexus-related update in the last 14 days

If any box is unchecked, fix it before outreach. Cold DMs from incomplete profiles get a 5% reply rate. Cold DMs from a complete founder profile get 25%.

## ✓ The first post to publish today

Don't wait for the perfect blog post. Publish this short version today:

```
After [N] years building data systems at [previous companies], I'm
founding DataNexus — an open-source data platform for India.

The problem: DPDP Act 2023 requires every Indian company holding citizen
data to prove compliance to regulators. AWS, GCP, and Azure cannot
legally provide this proof — their data routes through US servers.

The solution: DataNexus runs on customer infrastructure (cloud or
on-prem), records every data transformation on Hyperledger Fabric,
and generates compliance reports in 60 seconds with cryptographic
proof any regulator can verify.

Currently in customer discovery. If you lead data engineering or
compliance at an Indian bank, hospital, manufacturer, or government
department — I would love 15 minutes to learn what your real pains
look like. Comment below or DM me.

#DPDP #DataEngineering #Hyperledger #IndianStartup #OpenSource
```

That's 130 words. Posts under 300 words get the most engagement on LinkedIn. Publish it. Don't perfect it.

## ✓ The mistake to avoid

Don't connect with everyone first, then do outreach later. **Combine the two.**

When you send a connection request to a prospect, write a 200-character note that's the start of a conversation. Use the templates in `DataNexus_Outreach_Templates.docx`. The connection request IS the first DM.
